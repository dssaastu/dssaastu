Short Answer Questions

1. What is the role of moral intuitions in ethical decision-making?

Moral intuitions provide immediate, instinctive responses to ethical situations, serving as a starting point that can be refined through critical reasoning.

2. How does the Fairness and Justice Approach differ from the Common Good Approach in ethical decision-making?

The Fairness and Justice Approach focuses on treating individuals equitably, while the Common Good Approach prioritizes the welfare of the entire community over individual concerns.

3. Explain the Argument from Enlightened Self-Interest as a reason for being moral.

This argument suggests that humans should be moral because ethical behavior ultimately benefits them personally, such as through improved relationships or long-term well-being.

4. What are the four areas to which morality applies?

Morality applies to religious morality, morality and nature, individual morality, and social morality.

5. Why is critical reasoning important in testing moral arguments?

Critical reasoning is essential to evaluate the logic, consistency, and validity of moral arguments, ensuring they are not based solely on emotion or flawed assumptions.
