Short Answer Questions

1. Explain the difference between teleological and deontological ethics.

Teleological ethics judges morality by the consequences of actions, aiming for outcomes like happiness, while deontological ethics focuses on duty and rules, judging actions by their adherence to moral principles regardless of outcomes.

2. How does virtue ethics differ from utilitarianism?

Virtue ethics emphasizes developing moral character and virtues for a good life, whereas utilitarianism focuses on maximizing happiness through the consequences of actions, prioritizing outcomes over personal traits.

3. What is the main idea behind Kant’s categorical imperative?

The main idea is to act only according to rules that could be universally applied, treating people as ends in themselves, not as means to an end, based on reason and duty.

4. Why might utilitarianism be seen as controversial?

It can be controversial because it may justify actions that harm minorities or individuals if they benefit the majority, raising concerns about fairness and justice.

5. What is the role of the "golden mean" in Aristotle’s ethics?

The "golden mean" is the idea of finding a balanced middle ground between extremes of excess and deficiency in virtues, like courage between recklessness and cowardice, to achieve moral excellence.
